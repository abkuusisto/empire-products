Data Processing Agreement

Last updated: 1 July 2026
Processor: AB Empire Oy, Business ID 3499735-5, Finland

This Data Processing Agreement (“DPA”) forms part of the agreement between AB Empire Oy (“Processor”, “we”) and the customer using StoreGuard (“Controller”, “you”). It governs the processing of personal data carried out by AB Empire Oy on behalf of the Controller in connection with the StoreGuard service.

This DPA supplements the Terms of Service and, in the event of conflict regarding data processing matters, this DPA prevails.

1. Parties

Processor: AB Empire Oy, Business ID 3499735-5, Finland, operating as Qsisto.
Controller: The customer who has subscribed to StoreGuard and accepted the Terms of Service.

2. Subject Matter and Nature of Processing

The Processor provides Shopify store health monitoring under the StoreGuard service. Processing activities carried out on behalf of the Controller include:

  • Performing read-only HTTP requests against the Controller’s publicly accessible store URL to retrieve health signals.
  • Storing scan results, health scores, alert records, and trend data in secure databases.
  • Delivering email alerts and reports to the Controller.
  • Managing subscription and account records associated with the Controller.

The Processor does not access or process personal data of the Controller’s end customers through StoreGuard scanning. StoreGuard reads only publicly accessible HTML, the same as any anonymous browser session.

3. Categories of Data Subjects and Personal Data

Data subjects: The Controller (store operator or account holder).

Categories of personal data processed:

  • Email address and account credentials.
  • Shopify store URL.
  • Billing identifiers (managed through Stripe).
  • Scan event metadata (timestamps, response codes, detected signals).
  • Technical identifiers (IP address, session data, access logs).

4. Purpose and Duration of Processing

Personal data is processed solely for the purpose of delivering the StoreGuard service as described in the Terms of Service. Processing continues for the duration of the active subscription and for a reasonable period thereafter as required for legal compliance, dispute resolution, and account closure.

5. Processor Obligations

The Processor agrees to:

  • Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller, as set out in these Terms and DPA, unless required otherwise by applicable law.
  • Ensure that all personnel with access to personal data are bound by appropriate confidentiality obligations.
  • Implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.
  • Assist the Controller in responding to data subject rights requests (access, rectification, erasure, portability, restriction, objection) within a reasonable timeframe.
  • Notify the Controller without undue delay of any personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of natural persons.
  • Delete or return all personal data to the Controller upon request at the end of the service relationship, except where retention is required by applicable law.
  • Make available to the Controller all information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, and allow for and contribute to audits where required by applicable law.

6. Security Measures

The Processor maintains the following technical and organisational security measures:

  • Encrypted data transmission using HTTPS/TLS for all connections.
  • Access controls and role-based authentication limiting data access to authorised personnel.
  • Secure, access-controlled database infrastructure.
  • Security monitoring and incident response procedures.
  • Regular review of security practices.

7. Sub-processors

The Controller authorises the use of the following sub-processors:

  • Stripe, Inc. (United States): Payment processing for subscription billing.
  • Supabase, Inc. (United States): Database hosting and backend infrastructure.
  • Email delivery providers: Used for alert and notification delivery.

The Processor will notify the Controller of any intended additions or replacements of sub-processors with reasonable notice, providing the Controller the opportunity to object to such changes.

8. International Data Transfers

Some sub-processors may process data outside the European Economic Area. Where such transfers occur, the Processor ensures that appropriate safeguards are in place in accordance with GDPR Chapter V, including reliance on Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions, or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

9. Data Subject Rights

The Processor will assist the Controller in fulfilling its obligations to respond to requests from data subjects exercising their rights under applicable data protection law. The Controller remains responsible for responding to such requests. Requests should be directed to support@qsisto.com.
