What we check

Every scan covers these signals

Homepage reachability

Is your store loading? We check for HTTP 200 and fast response time.

HTTPS & SSL validity

Expired SSL can silently block checkout in modern browsers.

Shopify platform detection

Confirms your store is running Shopify and core assets are loading correctly.

Cart path accessible

We verify /cart returns a valid response as a proxy for cart functionality.

Checkout signals

Checks for Shopify checkout script presence and cart action signals in your page HTML.

Trust signals

Refund policy, shipping info, and trust badge presence — key conversion elements for first-time visitors.

Response time

Slow stores lose mobile shoppers. We track load speed and flag week-over-week regressions.

Week-over-week diff

Every check is compared to last week's scan. We surface regressions, not just outright failures.

What we do not check

Transparent about scope

StoreGuard is a lightweight external monitor — not a full QA suite. We never access your Shopify admin, place test orders, or log into anything.

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need to install a Shopify app?

No. StoreGuard works entirely from the outside. We run a read-only scan against your public store URL — the same way a customer browses your site. Nothing is installed in your Shopify admin.

Do you access my Shopify admin?

Never. We have no access to your Shopify backend, orders, customers, or settings. We only read your public-facing HTML, exactly as any browser would.

Do you complete test purchases?

No. We don't add items to cart, submit payment forms, or initiate any transaction. Our scan is entirely read-only and costs you nothing in Stripe fees or inventory.

What happens if an issue is found?

You receive an email alert immediately with the specific check that changed and what we recommend investigating first. You take action — we don't make any changes to your store.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. No annual commitments or lock-in. Cancel from your account or email support@qsisto.com and we'll confirm within one business day.

Is this a replacement for full QA testing?

No. StoreGuard is an early-warning monitoring layer. Think of it as your store's smoke alarm — not a full fire suppression system. For major launches, dedicated QA is still recommended.
